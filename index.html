Introduction to HCI

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is a field concerned with the design, evaluation, and implementation of interactive computing systems for human use and with the study of major phenomena surrounding them. It involves understanding the needs and capabilities of users and creating effective, efficient, and enjoyable user experiences. This involves considering various factors such as user needs, preferences, behaviors, and cognitive processes.

HCI encompasses disciplines such as psychology, design, computer science, and engineering, and it plays a crucial role in shaping the development of user-friendly technology and improving user experiences across different domains and applications.



HCI Techniques

There are various techniques used in HCI to design and evaluate interactive systems, including:

  • User Research
  • Usability Testing
  • Prototyping
  • User Interface Design
  • Information Visualization

Applications of HCI

HCI has many real-world applications across different domains, including:

  • Human-Robot Interaction
  • Healthcare Systems
  • E-Learning Platforms
  • Smart Homes
  • Virtual Reality
